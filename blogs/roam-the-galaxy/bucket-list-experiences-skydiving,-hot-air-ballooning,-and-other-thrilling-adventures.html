Bucket List Experiences: Skydiving, Hot Air Ballooning, and Other Thrilling Adventures

For me, the greatest thrill of traveling isn't just about seeing new places - it's about the incredible adventures and once-in-a-lifetime experiences you can have along the way. I'm an adrenaline junkie at heart, always seeking out the next epic, heart-poundingActivity to get my blood pumping. From skydiving and bungee jumping to scuba diving with sharks, I've checked off some amazing bucket list feats during my travels around the world. Each exhilarating plunge builds more courage and craving for my next adrenaline-rush.

For those who are true adventure seekers like me, here are some of my top recommended bucket list experiences, plus insider tips to make the most of these thrills when you find yourself somewhere new.

Skydiving: Jumping Out of a Perfectly Good Plane

Let's start with one of the biggest and most classic adrenaline adventures - skydiving! I'll never forget my first time leaping out of a "perfectly good plane" thousands of feet above the ground. It was in Australia, over the jaw-droppingly blue waters and endless sandy beaches of the Gold Coast. As I sat on the floor of the small aircraft, my stomach doing somersaults thinking "Why am I doing this?!", the moment finally came to shuffle to the open doorway. In two sweeps, my tandem instructor and I launched into the great abyss of open sky together.

The free fall experience is unlike any other - those first 60 seconds of pure adrenaline as you plummet over 120 mph will have you hollering in terror and euphoria. Then the miraculous parachute deploys, slowing down your descent to a peaceful cruising float, safe to take in the magnificent aerial views all around you. The landing is a bit rough but by then you're so high on the rush that nothing can wipe away your surge of pride and elation. I had such a blast that I made sure to skydive again in Fiji, soaring over the postcard-perfect South Pacific islands below.

Insider tip: While skydiving may seem like a spur-of-the-moment activity, be sure to thoroughly research and book with a reputable, highly experienced skydiving company, especially in less regulated countries or remote areas. Safety standards and gear inspections should be top priorities before you jump.

Hot Air Ballooning: Floating Serenely Above the World

For a more tranquil (but still wildly exhilarating!) adventure, hot air ballooning is hands down one of my all-time favorite bucket list experiences. You know that idyllic, rainbow-colored hot air balloon image you've dreamed about since childhood? Trust me, the real experience is even more wondrous than you can possibly imagine. I've been fortunate to go hot air ballooning in several bucket list destinations around the world - soaring over the ancient temples of Bagan, the wineries of Napa Valley, and most stunningly the golden sunrise over the Serengeti.

While the balloon gently lifts off the ground with the powerful heat blasts, you can instantly feel your body flood with a childlike sense of magic and buoyant bliss. There's no experience quite like peacefully floating up into the sky, high above the world below. It's a perfect panorama as the horizon stretches in every direction, the landscape transforming into a captivating patchwork quilt of colors and textures. For me, being up in a hot air balloon is the ultimate feeling of freedom and lightness. I'll never get tired of the awe-inspiring perspective from thousands of feet up!

Insider tip: Hot air ballooning is highly weather dependent, so you'll want to build in some flexibility in your schedule in case of rescheduling due to unexpected wind conditions or rain. Also be prepared for some very early morning wake up calls - most balloons try to launch around sunrise when the air is calmest. But trust me, being up in that glorious morning light makes the early wake up call completely worth it.

Diving with Sharks and Marine Life

While heights aren't an issue for this adventure junkie, facing my fear of deep waters was another beast I had to conquer when it came to scuba diving and underwater explorations. But seeing as I've always been fascinated by marine life and the incredible creatures lurking under the sea's surface, I couldn't keep putting it off. So for my very first open water scuba certification, I took the plunge (literally!) and did an advanced diving course in Bali. From there I've been hooked on finding new destinations to scuba and snorkel.

One of the most unforgettable moments in my life was cage diving with great white sharks off the coast of South Africa. Coming face-to-face with these powerful apex predators in their own domain is the kind of bone-chilling adrenaline you can't find anywhere else. Watching the massive beasts confidently glide up, metallically eyeing you through row after row of razor-sharp teeth - it's the stuff of nightmares. But it's also an incredible display of the majesty and might of these misunderstood giants. I emerged from that cage with a profound respect and sense of humility towards the natural world.

Since then, I've continued seeking out incredible diving experiences everywhere from Indonesia's Raja Ampat to the Bahamas' swimming pigs to witnessing massive manta ray migrations in Hawaii. Being able to quietly observe these magnificent, vibrantly-colored marine creatures up close in their own habitats is an adventure of the mind and soul.

Insider tip: to avoid ruining the precious underwater environments you'll be exploring, choose operators with a focus on conservation and sustainability. Avoid tours or providers that may promote touching or interacting with wildlife. As tempting as it sounds, the reality is this kind of interference can disrupt feeding patterns, breeding behaviors and natural activities. Always follow guidelines to admire from a respectful distance.

Hiking, Climbing, Caving and Other Epic Outdoor Adventures

From volcano boarding to canyoneering to ice climbing, I've taken on plenty of other pulse-pounding outdoor adventures around the world too. Anytime I feel my limits have gotten a little too comfortable, I challenge myself with a new white-knuckle pursuit - rappelling down towering cliffs, summiting formidable peaks, spelunking through underground cave systems, or even mushing up a glacier on a dog sled. Sure, these activities often leave me battered, bruised and physically spent by the end. But there's no better way to face your fears, celebrate your strength and feel fully alive.

On the exhilarating Navajo Circuit hike through Havasupai Falls, I took more inadvertent tumbles than I can count as I made my way up treacherously steep switchbacks, slick with moisture from the wet canyons below. While exploring Hang Son Doong, the world's largest known cave in Vietnam, I had to squeeze through knife-edge paths and pull myself across terrifyingly deep underground river passages, knowing any slip could mean disaster. But making it to the other side, I couldn't have felt more accomplished, proud of myself or grateful to have witnessed such raw, primal beauty.

Insider tip: Adventures like these require more extensive preparation, the right gear, and guided expertise. But the high risks make the reward that much sweeter, not to mention the lifelong bragging rights! Just make sure to follow all local rules and listen carefully to instructions from experienced guides/operators. They'll know the safest and most reputable ways to push your limits while staying protected.

Celebration piñatas, sparklers at kids parties, excessive use of fireworks - these kinds of activities may seem harmless enough at first glance. But they're often banned or regulated due to sustainability and safety concerns. Piñata debris doesn't just magically disappear, it litters the streets or natural environment, harming wildlife. Fireworks also pose major fire risks in dry climates and disrupt nearby animal habitats, not to mention the toxic fumes and heavy metals from explosives that pollute the air. For children's celebrations, it's better to avoid these outdated traditions altogether.

At the end of the day, some of the most meaningful travel memories come from facing your fears, pushing your limits and having your breath taken away by the power and beauty of nature. I'm still working on conquering new adventures to keep leveling up life's excitement. Next up, I've got my sights set on summiting Kilimanjaro, volcano boarding in Nicaragua, and going on a multi-day rafting descent through the Grand Canyon. Who's ready to join me?!

Life is short and this big, wild world is endlessly inspiring. So dream big, take that leap of faith and experience every thrill this planet has to offer! That's how you'll get the most juice out of this crazy adventure we call life.

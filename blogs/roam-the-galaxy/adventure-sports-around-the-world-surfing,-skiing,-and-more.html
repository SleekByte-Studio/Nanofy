What Is an Eco-Friendly Hotel? Defining Green Lodging

Looking for an eco-friendly place to rest your head on your next trip? You’re in the right place. This guide will give you the inside scoop on green hotels, eco lodges, and other sustainable stays across the country. We’re talking solar panels, LEED certification, locally-sourced dining—all the earth-friendly amenities you need to travel guilt-free. Whether you’re a hardcore environmentalist or just want to reduce your carbon footprint on your next getaway, we’ve got you covered. We’ll introduce you to accommodations of all styles and budgets that are walking the talk when it comes to sustainability. Get ready to explore the world of eco-conscious hospitality.

Benefits of Staying at a Sustainable Hotel or Eco Lodge

Sustainable Practices

  • Using renewable energy sources such as solar panels or geothermal heating.
  • Implementing water conservation measures like low-flow showerheads and toilets.
  • Serving locally-sourced, organic food.
  • Using non-toxic and biodegradable cleaning products.
  • Donating unused amenities to local charities.
  • Offering recycling and composting for guests.

Certifications

Many eco-friendly hotels obtain certifications to prove their green credentials. The most well-known is the Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design or LEED certification. Other certifications include Green Key, Green Seal, and Energy Star. These certifications indicate the hotel meets high standards for sustainability, energy efficiency, and reduced environmental impact.

Why Choose an Eco-Friendly Hotel?

By staying at an eco-friendly hotel, you can reduce your own environmental footprint during travel. You'll be supporting a business committed to sustainable and ethical practices. An eco-friendly hotel may also allow you to experience more authentic aspects of a destination by highlighting locally-sourced goods, culture, and natural surroundings. Overall, green lodging allows you to go green on vacation and still enjoy all the usual comforts of a hotel.

So next time you're booking accommodation, consider choosing an eco-friendly hotel. You'll rest easy knowing your stay treads lightly on our planet.

How to Identify Truly Green Hotels: Certifications and Credibility

Support Environmental Preservation

By choosing an eco-friendly hotel, you directly support their mission to implement sustainable practices that reduce waste and carbon emissions. Your stay helps fund things like solar panels, water conservation systems, and green space preservation. Every step they take to reduce environmental impact makes a difference.

Experience Natural Beauty

Many sustainable hotels and lodges are situated in gorgeous natural settings, often in remote locations that have been spared from overdevelopment. You may find yourself surrounded by lush rainforests, scenic mountains or pristine beaches. Waking up to those views is a perfect way to reconnect with nature.

Learn Valuable Lessons

Eco-lodges aim to educate guests about environmental responsibility and often offer programs on topics like reducing waste or supporting local communities. You can learn practical tips for more sustainable living that you can apply at home. Interacting with staff and like-minded guests also exposes you to new perspectives on how we can all work together to protect our planet.

Support Local Communities

Sustainable hotels frequently employ staff from nearby communities and use local suppliers for food, crafts and other goods. By staying there, you directly contribute to the local economy. Some eco-lodges also donate a portion of profits to community development programs or allow guests to sponsor local students. It's a meaningful way to give back during your travels.

Overall, choosing an eco-friendly hotel or lodge for your next vacation can be highly rewarding. You'll come home refreshed, enlightened and inspired to make a difference. What could be better than that?

Top 10 Eco-Friendly Hotels and Lodges in the US

Look for Official Certifications

  • Green Key - Awards 1-5 Green Keys based on over 200 criteria related to waste, energy, and environmental initiatives.
  • LEED - Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design certifies buildings that meet high standards for green design and construction. Look for LEED Silver, Gold or Platinum.
  • Green Globe - Certifies sustainable tourism businesses and their supply chain partners based on over 300 indicators.

Check Their Eco-Friendly Policies

  • They reduce energy usage through sensors, efficient appliances, and renewable energy.
  • They reduce waste by minimizing single-use plastics, composting food scraps, and recycling.
  • They conserve water through low-flow fixtures, greywater systems, and linen/towel reuse programs.
  • They use eco-friendly and organic products for cleaning, bath amenities, and food.
  • They give back to local communities and environment through donations or volunteer programs.

Read Reviews From Other Eco-Travelers

See what other environmentally-conscious travelers say about the hotel. Look for mentions of their sustainability initiatives, use of local and organic products, and overall “green vibe.” Be wary of hotels that use eco-friendly terms to market themselves but lack substance or certifications to back up these claims. The greenest hotels will have overwhelmingly positive reviews on sites like TripAdvisor, Yelp or the hotel’s Facebook page.

By checking certifications, examining policies, and reading reviews from fellow eco-travelers, you can determine which hotels are truly committed to sustainability. Give your business and support to hotels making a real difference, so together we can shape a greener future for travel.

How to Have an Environmentally Friendly Stay: Tips for Travelers

The Lodge at Chama, New Mexico

Nestled in the Sangre de Cristo Mountains, The Lodge at Chama is a rustic yet luxurious eco-lodge. It utilizes sustainable building practices and renewable energy like solar panels. The lodge’s restaurant sources local, organic ingredients and the spa uses all-natural products. With just 15 rooms, the small size allows for a personalized experience while reducing the lodge’s environmental footprint.

The Shore Hotel, Santa Monica, California

Overlooking the Santa Monica Pier, The Shore Hotel is a beachfront eco-friendly hotel. It has a silver LEED certification for its sustainable practices like recycling, composting, and water conservation. The hotel sources sustainable materials and partners with community organizations on environmental initiatives. They also offer electric vehicle charging stations and bike rentals for guests.

Bardessono, Yountville, California

In the heart of Napa Valley wine country sits Bardessono, a LEED platinum-certified eco-luxury hotel. It utilizes geothermal heating and cooling, solar panels, organic linens, and recycled materials. The hotel has an on-site organic garden providing produce for its gourmet restaurant and spa. Bardessono also practices water conservation, reducing usage by over 30% compared to comparable hotels.

The Umstead Hotel and Spa, Cary, North Carolina

Set on six acres of woodlands within a private, residential community, The Umstead Hotel and Spa is an eco-conscious luxury retreat. The Umstead employs sustainable practices like reducing energy usage, recycling, and sourcing local, organic ingredients. Its spa uses all natural and organic products. The Umstead’s three restaurants utilize herbs and vegetables from its on-site garden and source sustainable seafood and meats.

The Inn at the Presidio, San Francisco, California

Located in a historic building within San Francisco’s Presidio national park, this eco-friendly inn emphasizes sustainability. It utilizes renewable energy, sustainable building materials, and water conservation techniques. The inn’s restaurant focuses on locally-sourced organic ingredients. A portion of profits also go toward supporting the Golden Gate National Parks Conservancy's environmental and community programs. The inn’s natural setting in the Presidio, surrounded by gardens and hiking trails, provides a peaceful escape in the city.

Hang Ten! The Best Surfing Spots Around the World

You're always seeking that adrenaline rush, eager for the next outdoor challenge to get your heart pounding. Forget lounging on the beach - you want to hang ten on those waves! While others ski the groomed slopes, you're eyeing that backcountry terrain. You crave the freedom of flying off jumps on your mountain bike, pushing your limits. Get ready for an inside look at some of the top adventure sports spanning the globe that will satisfy your appetite for action. From surfing tropical breaks to heli-skiing remote peaks, we've got you covered with the ultimate guide to must-do sports for thrill-seekers ready to chase the thrill of victory and the agony of defeat. You won't find armchair adventures here. This is for those yearning to push boundaries and expand horizons through sweat, grit, and determination. Adventure awaits. Are you ready to answer the call?

Hit the Slopes! Top Skiing and Snowboarding Destinations

Pipeline, North Shore of Oahu, Hawaii

No list of legendary surf spots is complete without Pipeline. The massive barrels here are the stuff of surfing dreams. In the winter, swells can reach up to 60 feet, though 10 to 15 feet is more common. Either way, this is not a wave for beginners. If you've got the skills, the rewards are worth it - just watch out for the razor-sharp reef below.

Supertubes, Jeffrey's Bay, South Africa

If you prefer riding longer, more consistent barrels, head to Jeffrey's Bay in South Africa. The waves here seem to go on forever, some lasting up to a quarter mile. The break is fast, powerful, and not too crowded, especially if you come in the off-season. For the ultimate thrill, hit the wave during a competition and watch the pros do their thing.

Cloud Nine, Siargao Island, Philippines

For a taste of paradise, check out Cloud Nine in the Philippines. This secluded reef break offers huge, hollow barrels and stunning turquoise water. After your session, relax in a beachfront bungalow or sample fresh seafood at a local cafe. The vibe here is laid-back, the crowds are minimal, and the surf is unreal. What more could you want?

Rincon, Santa Barbara, California

Just off the coast of Santa Barbara sits one of California's most iconic waves. Rincon is a fast, powerful reef break that churns out thick, barreling rights - considered by some to be the longest rideable waves in the state. The water is cold, the rocks are sharp, and the locals can be territorial, but when the conditions are right, Rincon is a surfing nirvana. Timing is everything, so watch the forecasts and jump on a swell when one comes through.

Feel the Rush! White Water Rafting Hot Spots

The Swiss Alps, Switzerland

If you want to experience the stunning snow-capped peaks of the Swiss Alps, head to resorts like Zermatt, St. Moritz or Davos. Glide down over 200 miles of slopes while taking in the breathtaking views. Most resorts offer terrain for all skill levels, as well as plenty of off-piste skiing for thrill-seekers. Après-ski activities like shopping, dining and nightlife abound in the charming alpine villages.

Whistler Blackcomb, British Columbia, Canada

Consistently ranked as one of the top ski resorts in North America, Whistler Blackcomb offers over 8,000 acres of slopes across 16 alpine bowls and over 200 marked runs. The resort gets an average of 459 inches of snowfall each year, so conditions are usually prime for skiing and snowboarding. Two gondolas and 37 lifts help you access terrain for all abilities. When you're done on the slopes, explore the pedestrian village with shops, restaurants, bars and nightclubs.

Queenstown, New Zealand

New Zealand's stunning natural scenery provides the perfect backdrop for an exciting snow adventure. The resort town of Queenstown on the South Island is a top ski destination, offering slopes for all skill levels. It's home to four ski fields, including The Remarkables and Coronet Peak, which offer over 200 ski runs combined. Queenstown is also a popular base for heli-skiing, where helicopters drop you on pristine snowfields for a true thrill ride. After a day of adventure, enjoy the vibrant nightlife, fine dining and stunning lake and mountain views.

Reach New Heights! Rock Climbing and Mountaineering Adventures

The Zambezi River, Zimbabwe/Zambia

For thrill seekers, the Zambezi River offers some of the most intense rafting in the world. As one of the wildest rivers in Africa, the Zambezi features over 20 rapids ranging from grade 3 to 5, including the mighty grade 5 “Stairway to Heaven.” The scenic gorges and thundering rapids will get your adrenaline pumping. Trips run from half day to multi-day, with scenic camping under the stars.

The Futaleufú River, Chile

Nestled in the Andes Mountains, the Futaleufú River is renowned for its pristine turquoise waters and challenging rapids. With over 40 rapids ranging from grade 3 to 5, the Futaleufú offers an exciting ride for experienced rafters. The grade 5 “Throne Room” rapid culminates in a massive 15-foot waterfall—if you make it through upright! The stunning scenery and secluded campsites make the Futaleufú a rafter’s paradise.

The Gauley River, West Virginia

For a taste of world-class rafting in the U.S., head to the Gauley River in West Virginia. Dropping over 600 feet in just 24 miles, the Gauley features over 100 rapids, including over a dozen class IV-V rapids. The 16-mile Upper Gauley run is one of the most popular, including the legendary “Sweet's Falls.” The Gauley season only runs six weeks in fall, when summertime dams release water from the Summersville Lake reservoir. If you want to experience the Gauley at its most extreme, go during the first few weeks of the release for the biggest rapids.

With thrilling rapids surrounded by stunning scenery, these white water rafting destinations will satisfy your thirst for adventure. But be prepared—once you experience the rush of the rapids, you’ll be hooked!

Frequently Asked Questions About Adventure Sports Travel

Rock Climbing

Whether you prefer climbing natural rock faces or artificial climbing walls, this sport tests your strength, flexibility, and problem-solving skills. Popular rock climbing destinations include Yosemite National Park, the Shawangunk Mountains in New York, and Red River Gorge in Kentucky. Indoor climbing gyms are also a great way to learn the ropes. You'll need a harness, climbing shoes, chalk for your hands, and a helmet for safety.

Mountaineering

For thrill-seekers looking to summit majestic mountain peaks, mountaineering is the ultimate challenge. Some of the most famous mountaineering destinations include Denali in Alaska, Aconcagua in Argentina, and Mount Kilimanjaro in Tanzania. You'll need high-quality gear like thermal layers, mountaineering boots, ice axes, ropes, tents, and more to conquer these extreme elevations. Mountaineering requires extensive training to prepare for the technical skills and dangers involved.

Whether you're a novice climber or seasoned mountaineer, these sports offer exhilarating adventures in stunning natural environments. While the climbs and treks can be strenuous, the rewards of reaching a summit or sending a challenging route are well worth it. Adventure awaits at high altitudes, so grab your gear and get ready to scale new heights!
